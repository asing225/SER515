ABOUT

The vision for Drag-On is that this project is a fully functional stand-alone application to aid students from grades 1 to 6 in applying arithmetic expressions and evaluating their functionality using visual programming elements such as modules and blocks.


Drag-On is a visual aided playground specifically developed for children aged between 6 to 16, in order to help them strengthen their understanding of standard arithmetic expression.


This project extends its imagination from that of SCRATCH by MIT (https://scratch.mit.edu/) which shares a similar goal in developing a creative environment for learning.



This application delivers a system that can be used by an entity such as a school, where the Drag-On playground is available for students to learn and the teachers can use this application to create quizzes or the students to learn and grow.

Our target audience are students of Grade 1 and Grade 6. This demographic ensures the diversity in the age group of students and will help cater to the idea of expansion of the application for students of all grades.

LEARNING OUTCOMES FOR GRADE 1

In first grade math, students will be expected to:


  • and write numerals to 100 and to count objects to 100 or more
  • Learn ordinal numbers from 1st to 10th or higher

Counting is an important skill for a first-grade math student. The children will learn to:


  • Count orally to 100 by 2s, 5s, and 10s with or without a hundred chart
  • Count forward or backward by 1s beginning with any number less than 100

First-grade math students will be learning about place value including:


  • The place value of any digit in a whole number less than 100
  • Place value patterns and how zero is a placeholder for expressing place value

During this year, first-grade math students will learn addition and subtraction. The children will demonstrate that they know addition means putting together, subtraction means taking away, and the following:


  • Learn about the commutative and associative properties of addition and learn fact families up to 18
  • Taught about simple fractions, learning that equivalent fraction parts make a whole

Algebraic Thinking:


Children in first-grade math will predict, extend, and create patterns. They will:


  • Solve addition and subtraction sentences where an unknown number is represented by a geometric shape
  • Use objects to solve number sentences with equalities and inequalities using <, =, and >


LEARNING OUTCOMES FOR GRADE 6

In sixth grade math, students will be expected to:


  • Students will use roman numerals to solve problems
  • Apply strategies in order to solve real-life problems
  • Round, compare, and order whole numbers up to one trillion

Basic arithmetic is an important skill for sixth-grade math. The children will learn to:


  • Students will apply the order of operations in order to solve problems
  • Students will use variables in order to simplify expressions

Sixth-grade math students will be learning about fractions and decimals including:


  • Students will compare and order fractions including using a number line
  • Adding, subtracting, multiplying, and dividing fractions with like and unlike denominators

Sixth-grade math students will be learning about integers and graphing which includes:

  • Adding, subtracting, multiplying and dividing using integers
  • Students will use deductive or inductive reasoning, process of elimination or work backward to solve real-life problems

Algebraic Thinking:


Sixth-grade math students will be learning about integers and graphing which includes:


  • Students will use integers to describe real-life situations and show them on a number line
  • Adding, subtracting, multiplying and dividing using integers
